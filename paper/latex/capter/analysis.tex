Based on quantitative evaluation and manual inspection, we identify key factors that explain model failures.

\subsection{Non-scalable Decision-Making Capability}

\input{table/analysis_sales}

Models achieve reasonable performance in Easy settings but degrade as complexity increases (Appendix~\ref{appendix:total_results}). We analyze SKUs and categories sold per day versus those considered during strategy phases (Tables~\ref{tab:sold_sku_cat_context}, Appendix~\ref{appendix:category_analysis}). Decision-making capability does not scale proportionally with environment size; performance remains flat despite more options. Notably, Gemini-3 (Fast), with the largest context window, performs better, suggesting context capacity helps retain salient information. Even the strongest models fail to cover the full decision space, leading to systematic degradation in larger settings.

\subsection{Incomplete Information Coverage}

\input{table/tool_usage_frequency_easy}

We analyze information sources models query for SKUs in daily strategies. Most models primarily rely on supplier prices, inventory levels, SKU ratings, and historical sales (Table~\ref{tab:tool_usage_easy}), while consistently ignoring critical signals like customer reviews, return rates, and current prices. Correlation analysis shows strong positive relationships between review query frequency and sales performance (Appendix~\ref{appendix:tool_use_analysis}), indicating incomplete information coverage limits decision quality.

\subsection{Temporal Instability}

\input{table/strategy_stability}

Beyond scalability and information coverage, temporal instability contributes to long-horizon failure. Agents frequently revise strategies across consecutive days, even under stable conditions. We quantify instability via macro strategy similarity (LLM-based semantic consistency) and execution strategy similarity (Jaccard similarity over key fields), computing Std\_diff, MAC, and TV metrics. Across all environments, we observe consistent temporal instability (Table~\ref{tab:strategy_instability_easy}, Appendix~\ref{appendix:score_analysis}): macro strategies remain relatively stable, but execution strategies show substantial variability—especially for Qwen-235B (Thinking). Failures arise from inability to maintain consistent execution policies over extended horizons.
\subsection{Hallucinations and Invalid Actions}

Manual inspection reveals recurrent patterns breaking correctness and validity. First, models hallucinate non-existent SKUs or quantities, incorporating these into plans (Appendix~\ref{appendix:hallucinations}), misaligning decisions with true state. Second, models output invalid actions violating constraints (negative quantities, implausible pricing; Appendix~\ref{appendix:invalid_action}). These occur frequently and can destabilize systems. In realistic settings, both would be unacceptable and could trigger collapse.
